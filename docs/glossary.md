# Music Glossary

A quick reference for musical terms you might see across the website.

## Core Song Structure

### Verse
The main section of a song where the story or lyrics progress. Verses often share the same melody but use different words.

### Chorus
A repeated section of a song, usually featuring the main hook or message. The chorus is typically the most memorable part.

### Bridge
A contrasting section that connects parts of a song (often verse and chorus) and adds variety before returning to a familiar section.

### Hook
A catchy musical or lyrical idea that grabs the listener’s attention. Hooks often appear in the chorus but can appear anywhere.

### Outro
The closing section of a song, used to wind things down or provide a final musical statement.

## Rhythm & Tempo

### Tempo
The speed of a song, usually measured in beats per minute (B.P.M). Higher B.P.M means a faster song.

### BPM (Beats Per Minute)
A measurement of tempo that tells you how many beats occur in one minute of music.

### Groove
The overall rhythmic feel of a song, created by how the drums, bass and other instruments interact.

### Syncopation
A rhythmic effect created by emphasizing normally weak beats or off-beats, often making the music feel more energetic or unexpected.

## Melody & Harmony

### Riff
A short, memorable musical phrase—often played on guitar, bass or keys—that repeats and helps define the character of a song.

### Motif
A small musical idea or pattern that recurs throughout a piece, helping to create a sense of unity.

### Melody
The main tune of a song—the part you would usually sing or hum.

### Harmony
Notes played or sung at the same time as the melody to create chords and a fuller sound.

### Chord Progression
A series of chords played in a specific order. Progressions create the harmonic backbone of a song.

## Sound & Production

### Timbre
The unique “colour” or quality of a sound that helps you distinguish one instrument or voice from another, even when they play the same note.

### Dynamics
The loudness or softness of music over time. Changes in dynamics can make a song feel more emotional or dramatic.

### Mix
The way all the recorded parts of a song (vocals, drums, guitars etc.) are balanced and blended together in the final version.

### Fade-In/Fade-Out
When a track gradually increases from silence (fade-in) or decreases into silence (fade-out) instead of starting or stopping abruptly.

## Genre & Style Terms

### Subgenre
A more specific style within a broader genre (for example: math rock is a subgenre of rock; bebop is a subgenre of jazz).

### Crossover
Music that blends elements from two or more genres, often appealing to different audiences at the same time.

### Improvisation
Creating music on the spot rather than playing something completely planned. Improvisation is especially common in genres like jazz and blues.
