# How to Use All Things Music

This guide walks you through how to get around the All Things Music website, find songs, explore genres, and contribute your own content.

## Navigating the Website

The main ways to move around the website are:

- **Top bar** – shows the website title and a link back to the homepage.
- **Sidebar** – the primary navigation for all pages.
- **Page navigation** – previous/next links at the bottom of each page.

From any page, you can click the website logo or the **Introduction** link in the sidebar to return to the home page.

## Using the Sidebar

The sidebar groups related content so you can quickly jump to what you need:

- **Introduction** – the homepage with quick shortcuts to popular sections.
- **How to Use** – this guide, explaining how to work with the website.
- **About** – background on the project and its goals.
- **Genres** – expandable section listing each supported genre (Blues, Classical, Electronic, Folk, Hip-Hop, Jazz, Pop, Rap, Rock).
- **Instruments** – links to instrument-focused pages (for example, Guitar).

Click any item in the sidebar to open that page. Collapsible groups (like **Genres**) can be expanded or collapsed to show or hide their children.

## Using the Search Feature

All Things Music includes a built-in search so you can quickly find songs, albums, or topics.

- Look for the **Search** input at the top of the page.
- Type in a keyword such as a **song title**, **artist name**, **genre** or **instrument**.
- Search results update to show pages whose titles or content match your query.
- Click a result to open that page directly.

Tips:

- Try different keywords (for example: an artist name instead of a song title) if you do not see what you expect.
- Short, specific terms usually work best.

## Browsing by Genre

To explore music by style:

1. Open the **Genres** section in the sidebar.
2. Choose a genre such as **Rock**, **Jazz**, or **Hip-Hop**.
3. On each genre page, scroll through the listed albums and songs.

Genre pages typically include:

- Brief descriptions of the genre.
- Tables or tabs listing songs, artists, albums, subgenres and years.

Use the links within these pages (where available) to jump between related songs, albums or artists.

## Exploring Instruments

Instrument pages focus on music organized around specific instruments.

To view instrument content:

1. In the sidebar, open the **Instruments** section.
2. Select an instrument, such as **Guitar**.
3. Browse the examples, songs or recommendations related to that instrument.

As the project grows, more instruments may be added to this section.

## Contributing to the Content

All Things Music is community-driven. You are encouraged to add new songs, albums or genres.

To contribute:

1. Visit the project’s GitHub repository: <https://github.com/NSCC-ITC-Winter2026-WEBD3030-700-MCa/All-things-Music>.
2. Read the [Contributing Guide](../CONTRIBUTING.md) for step-by-step instructions.
3. Review the [Templates](../templates.md) for songs and albums so your additions follow the same format.
4. Create or edit the appropriate Markdown file under the `docs/genres/` or `docs/instruments/` folders.
5. Submit a pull request with your changes.

Once your contribution is reviewed and merged, it will appear on the website after the next build and deployment.

## Getting Help

If you are unsure where something belongs or how to format it:

- Check existing pages (for example, a similar genre or instrument) and follow their structure.
- Refer back to this **How to Use** guide and the **About** and **Templates** pages.
- Open an issue on GitHub to ask questions or propose improvements.

Enjoy exploring and sharing music with the All Things Music community!
