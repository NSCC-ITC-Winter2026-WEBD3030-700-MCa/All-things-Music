# Blues

::: callout tip
The home base for all things blues music
:::

Blues is a music genre originating in the Deep South of the United States, rooted in African-American work songs, spirituals, and folk music. Known for its soulful expression of hardship and resilience, the blues laid the foundation for rock, jazz, and R&B.

## Little Red Rooster

::: card
**Little Red Rooster** by Howlin' Wolf (1961)

A raw, slow-tempo Chicago blues classic featuring Howlin' Wolf's growling vocals and Willie Dixon's iconic bass line. Later covered by The Rolling Stones.
:::

## The Thrill Is Gone

::: card
**The Thrill Is Gone** by B.B. King (1969)

One of B.B. King's signature songs, blending blues with orchestral strings. A timeless expression of heartbreak and loss.
:::

## Cross Road Blues

::: card
**Cross Road Blues** by Robert Johnson (1936)

A legendary Delta blues recording that inspired generations of rock and blues musicians, famously associated with the myth of Johnson selling his soul at the crossroads.
:::

## Hoochie Coochie Man

::: card
**Hoochie Coochie Man** by Muddy Waters (1954)

Written by Willie Dixon, this Chicago blues anthem became one of Muddy Waters' most iconic recordings, built on a powerful stop-time riff.
:::
